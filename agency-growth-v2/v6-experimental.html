What blocks growth.

The work is all there. The capability is often there too. But without the right clarity, packaging, and orchestration, growth stalls. These are the eight blockers I see most often.

1
Market readiness
Foundations aren't solid enough yet. Push sales or hiring too hard before that and you only amplify the cracks. Money gets spent. Progress doesn't stick.
2
Disconnected teams
Everything feels disconnected. Delivery teams are busy, but not feeding growth, and there's no shared view of what the agency is pushing towards.
3
No productised offer
There isn't a defined programme with a fixed outcome. It feels open-ended, which makes it harder to buy and harder for prospects to understand what they're saying yes to.
4
Core assets need tightening
Website, copy, case studies, proof points and pillars all exist, but the positioning isn't landing hard enough in a compelling way.
5
No orchestration capacity
There isn't enough time or bandwidth internally to align everything properly and drive it through. Good intentions are there, but momentum depends on luck.
6
Pace doesn't match ambition
Progress feels slower and lighter than it should be. The capability is there. The urgency isn't being matched with the same velocity.
7
Pipeline blind spot
No clear pipeline stages or definitions, and it's difficult to understand what is currently in play. Forecasting feels like guesswork.
8
Senior cost barriers
Senior growth thinking is required, but you can't commit £120k–£150k for a full-time role, especially in the current economic climate.
